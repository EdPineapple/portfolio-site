"I want to be in a world created by art, not just decorated by it." - Banksy

The beauty of art is what draws the attention of the viewer, but the meaning and story behind it is what makes them stay.

I'm Ed - a Toronto based UI and UX designer, digital artist and visual storyteller. My experiences have made me grow to understand the need for balance between art and meaning, beauty and practicality, aesthetics and functionality.

During my time studying game design, I specialized in digital art and user interfaces. Whether I was designing control systems, creating UI and HUD elements or rigorously bug and exploit testing, I always sought to create a seamless and immersive experience with the player at the forefront of all my design decisions. It was through this focus on player-centric design that my love of UI/UX was born.

I completed the CareerFoundry UX Design bootcamp to kickstart my transition into the world of user experience. Over the course of my studies, I learned about the entire design process. From competitive analyses and user personas, to wireframes and usability testing, I took an app idea all the way from conception to polished, high-fidelity prototype.

I then took a further specialization course in frontend development wherein I created my own portfolio site (the one you're on right now!) to learn the basics of web development and to help me better communicate and collaborate with developers down the line.

Outside of work, I draw inspiration from minimalism, meditation, fashion and cooking.

I never back down from a good challenge, and my competitive nature prompts me to constantly be learning and seeking out new experiences in both my personal and professional life. Whether that's learning a new board game to play with friends, or a new design tool, I always strive to bring excellence to any project I work on.
