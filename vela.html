Overview

Vela is a wind, wave and weather app for water sports aficionados, and provides a user-friendly experience for beginners and experts alike.

Vela helps its users find detailed and accurate weather reports, sign up for water sports lessons and set up custom alerts so they never miss a wave.

Design Process

Identifying the Problem

An app that provides simple, easy-to-understand wind, wave and weather
reports can serve users who may be intimidated by more complex weather
apps and help them enjoy a safe day out on the water.

Competitor Analysis

The first part of the discovery phase involved conducting competitor
analyses to help me better understand the UX of similar apps on the
market and how I could learn from and further improve on their designs,
offering an even better experience for my potential users with Vela.

User Research

I conducted interviews with my target audience to gather qualitative
data about my potential users' thoughts and behaviours regarding current
water sports-related weather apps on the market. My goals with user
research were to figure out what features my target audience would
prioritize and need from Vela, and in what situations they would need to
use it to accomplish their goals.

I used affinity mapping to group my test results into five distinct
clusters. Using these affinity map clusters, I was able to glean key insights
from my interviews which I used to guide further research.

Cluster

Insights

Learning Habits

Users tend to have a hard time
understanding complex weather
jargon related to their sport
when they are first starting out.
Users would benefit from
learning features included in a
weather app for water sports.

Usage Habits

Users tend to check their
weather apps on the day of or
the day before their water sport
sessions. They would benefit
from being able to see detailed
weather reports up to a couple
weeks in advance for trip
planning purposes.

Preferred Apps

Users tend to use the basic weather
app on their phone for all their
weather needs but are unsatisfied
with the lack of detailed wind
data. They are also unsatisfied
with how long it takes to get the
same information from other
sources such as radio or television.

App Pricing

Users are willing to pay for an
app if it looks professional and
provides all the features they
need. They are more likely to
pay for an app if they have had
an opportunity to try it first.

Community

Users would benefit from
some type of social
connectivity in a weather
app for water sports in
order to connect with
their local communities.

Personas

I created two personas to serve as a cumulative representation of all the
common user needs, goals, tasks and pain points that I derived from my
user interviews.

User Flows

After defining my personas, I worked on creating user flows to simulate
how they would interact with my app. I then mapped out the most
optimal way for them to complete their most common tasks.

Information Architecture

I created a sitemap to show the navigation layout of the app detail
all the pages I would need and how they connect to one another.

I then conducted an open card sort to further refine my sitemap and
organize it in a way that would be more intuitive to my users.

Ideation

With all the information I needed, I created mid-fidelity wireframes of
all the key screens necessary to complete the main user tasks that I would
be testing with my prototype.

Usability Testing

The goal of this study was to determine if Vela's current design was
understandable for new users and easy to learn. The study specifically
focused on testing the effectiveness of the app's three core features and
aimed to measure how well test participants performed at completing
tasks within the app.

A total of six moderated usability tests were conducted; half remotely
and half in-person. The sessions began with an introduction and some
background questions before moving into the actual testing of the
prototype. Finally, the sessions ended with a quick wrap-up where users
offered any final thouhts or feedback.

Review & Approval

After organizing all the usability test results into a rainbow spreadsheet,
I worked on making changes to the design based on the feedback I
received and the issues my participants faced with the current iteration of
the prototype.

Following these changes, I further continued to iterate on my design and
refine the UI of Vela, applying visual design principles and bringing the
design more in-line with iOS design standards.
