Overview

FlashNotes is a flashcard app that allows users to connect to and chat with friends, share notebooks and study together in short, head-to-head challenges to foster motivation and accountability in learning.

FlashNotes is a very social app by nature with features like chat and leaderboards being emphasized to encourage cooperative and competitive learning.

Role & Duration

Product Designer

UI Design, UX Design, User Research, Usability Testing

October 2019 to January 2020

Identifying the Problem

An app that provides customizable learning goals, structured content and social connectivity can serve users who learn best in social settings and help keep them motivated and accountable in their studies.

User Research & Proto-Persona

I conducted interviews with my target audience to gather qualitative data about how my potential users feel about current flashcard apps on the market and what kind of features they would want in a flashcard-based learning app.

With the data I gathered during my interviews, I created a proto-persona which served as a representation of all my interviewees' common needs, goals and pain points.

User Flows

I then created user flows to map out the most optimal way for users to complete core tasks.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

User flows in hand, I began sketching out low-fidelity paper wireframes. These sketches showcase early UI and navigation concepts.

Once I was happy with my paper wireframes, I converted them into an early digital prototype.

Usability Testing & High-Fidelity Prototype

After finishing the first draft of my prototype, I put together a usability test plan to determine how easily users could complete a few of FlashNotes' core tasks and navigate the app in general. I then conducted moderated usability tests with my target audience to test the effectiveness of my current design.

I organized my test results into a usability test report and used the findings to inform my next round of iterations and further refine the UI of FlashNotes.
